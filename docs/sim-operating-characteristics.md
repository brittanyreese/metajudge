# DIF operating characteristics

Simulation study of the shipped `logistic_dif` engine's Type-I error, power, and
diagnostic calibration. The multi-persona review of 2026-07-02
([docs/reviews/2026-07-02-multi-persona-review.md](reviews/2026-07-02-multi-persona-review.md))
found that these numbers existed only as pass/fail bounds inside opt-in slow tests;
this document reports them.

Provenance: generated by `scripts/run_oc_study.py` at commit `ab751d0` with the seeds
pinned in `sim/oc_study.py` (base seed 20260702, disjoint per-block streams). Raw
per-replication results are committed under `sim/results/`. Analytic cells use 400
Monte Carlo replications (binomial SE 0.011 at the nominal 0.05 rate); the bootstrap
block uses 50 replications per cell (SE 0.031) with 200 resamples each. The
data-generating process is the cumulative-logit model in `sim/dgp.py`; convergence was
at least 371 of 400 in every cell and at least 396 of 400 outside the most extreme
separation cells.

## S1: baseline Type-I and power

External conditioner, 100 items per group, 3 raters, alpha = 0.05.

| Cell | Type-I / power (total) | Uniform | Nonuniform | Mean R2 delta |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Null, no impact | 0.068 | 0.063 | 0.048 | 0.003 |
| Null, impact -0.5 | 0.045 | 0.045 | 0.045 | 0.003 |
| Null, impact -1.0 | 0.062 | 0.052 | 0.052 | 0.003 |
| Uniform DIF 0.5 | 0.852 | 0.908 | 0.055 | 0.017 |
| Uniform DIF 1.0 | 1.000 | 1.000 | 0.053 | 0.057 |
| Uniform DIF 1.5 | 1.000 | 1.000 | 0.048 | 0.120 |
| Nonuniform DIF 0.3 | 0.380 | 0.045 | 0.468 | 0.007 |

Type-I stays inside the two-binomial-SE band [0.025, 0.075] at every impact level when
the conditioner is valid. Power at the B-band boundary (uniform DIF 0.5) is 0.85 and
saturates from 1.0 upward. The null mean R2 delta (0.003) sits far below the
Jodoin-Gierl A/B boundary (0.035).

## S2: does the crossed design inflate the analytic test?

The cluster-bootstrap ADR motivates a robustness layer with the multilevel-DIF
literature (French and Finch 2010), which found analytic Type-I inflation under
clustered data. The review asked whether that inflation actually occurs in this
design. Null cells, external conditioner, crossing panel size, rater variance, and
per-group item count:

| Raters | Rater SD | Items/group | Analytic Type-I | Bootstrap CI false-positive |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 3 | 0.5 | 25 | 0.050 | 0.02 |
| 3 | 0.5 | 100 | 0.030 | 0.00 |
| 3 | 1.5 | 25 | 0.028 | 0.00 |
| 3 | 1.5 | 100 | 0.025 | 0.00 |
| 8 | 0.5 | 25 | 0.055 | 0.00 |
| 8 | 0.5 | 100 | 0.056 | 0.00 |
| 8 | 1.5 | 25 | 0.020 | 0.00 |
| 8 | 1.5 | 100 | 0.021 | 0.00 |

The finding is negative, and it is the reported result rather than a buried one: with
a valid external conditioner, the analytic Type-I never inflates in this crossed
design. If anything it turns conservative as rater clustering grows (0.020 at 8
raters, SD 1.5). The rest-score conditioner absorbs the shared item effect, and the
grouping variable lives at the item level, which is not the between-cluster
configuration where French and Finch found severe inflation. A within-cluster grouping
variant is not representable here because group is an item-level property by design.
The item-block bootstrap tracks the same behavior (false-positive rate at or below
0.02 everywhere). The bootstrap therefore earns its place as a robustness check, not
as a correction for an inflation this design does not produce; the residual
cross-item within-rater dependence it does not resample showed no measurable Type-I
cost in this grid.

One side observation feeds the small-N caveat that already ships: the null mean R2
delta rises from 0.002 at 100 items/group to 0.010 at 25, finite-sample inflation that
the existing n_obs warning on the Jodoin-Gierl classification correctly covers.

## S3: calibrating the conditioner-overlap flag

The confound ADR (2026-07-01) shipped `conditioner_overlap_weak` with an honest note
that its 0.7 threshold was "a project convention, not an empirically validated
cutoff." This study is the validation it called for. Null cells (no true DIF) in the
two confounded regimes: the rest-score conditioner under impact, and a degraded
external conditioner under impact.

Rest-score conditioner (per-cell, 400 reps):

| Raters | Impact | False-DIF rate (p < .05) | Mean observed \|corr\| | Old flag fired |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 3 | 0.0 | 0.065 | 0.05 | never |
| 3 | -0.5 | 0.418 | 0.15 | never |
| 3 | -1.0 | 0.982 | 0.29 | never |
| 3 | -1.5 | 1.000 | 0.41 | never |
| 3 | -2.0 | 1.000 | 0.52 | never |
| 8 | -1.0 | 0.954 | 0.38 | never |
| 8 | -2.0 | 1.000 | 0.64 | 5% of reps |

The degraded-external block behaves the same way (Type-I 0.269 to 1.000 as reliability
falls from 0.8 to 0.4 under impact, with the old flag firing in at most 4% of
replications). Two conclusions follow.

First, the p-value is unusable as a screen in the confounded regime: residual impurity
drives the false-DIF rate toward 1.0 well before the strata stop overlapping. This
quantifies, rather than merely states, why the report card leads with the effect-size
class and demotes analytic p-values.

Second, the 0.7 convention watched the wrong part of the scale. Observed
conditioner-group correlation rarely reaches 0.7 even at extreme strata separation
(mean 0.52 at impact -2.0 with 3 raters), so the flag essentially never fired in cells
whose false-DIF rate was already 100%.

Pooling every null replication from both blocks (6,317 converged reps) by the
correlation each run actually observed gives the calibration curve. Because the card
leads with the Jodoin-Gierl class, the operative error is a false B or C
classification under no true DIF:

| Observed \|corr\| | n | False-DIF rate (p) | Mean R2 delta | False B/C rate |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 0.0 to 0.2 | 1396 | 0.137 | 0.004 | 0.005 |
| 0.2 to 0.4 | 1927 | 0.792 | 0.021 | 0.166 |
| 0.4 to 0.5 | 1212 | 0.851 | 0.040 | 0.529 |
| 0.5 to 0.6 | 1066 | 0.974 | 0.040 | 0.524 |
| 0.6 to 0.7 | 682 | 0.893 | 0.019 | 0.075 |
| 0.7 to 0.8 | 34 | 0.853 | 0.017 | 0.000 |

Below 0.2 the classifier is safe (0.5% false B/C). Past 0.2 the false B/C rate is
already 17%, and past 0.4 the impurity inflates the mean effect size itself into the
B band (0.040 under a true null). The threshold accordingly moved from 0.7 to 0.2;
the decision record is
[2026-07-02-e07-overlap-threshold-calibration.md](decisions/2026-07-02-e07-overlap-threshold-calibration.md).
The `conditioner_common_support` range-overlap measure stayed above 0.9 in almost
every confounded cell, so it carries no calibration weight; it remains descriptive
output only.

Where the shipped demo sits on this curve: the SummEval card observes |corr| = 0.147
with common support 1.0, inside the safe band. The review's Devil's Advocate asked
whether the demo's class-A null might be the rest score absorbing a real
extractive-abstractive quality gap; at this observed overlap the measured false-class
risk is 0.5%, so the null reads as a genuine screen result, not an artifact of
absorption.

## S4: proportional-odds violation

PO violation crossed with DIF, external conditioner:

| PO violation | DIF | Type-I / power | Nonuniform rejection | Brant flag rate |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 0.3 | none | 0.040 | 0.042 | 0.152 |
| 0.3 | 1.0 | 1.000 | 0.065 | 0.158 |
| 0.6 | none | 0.055 | 0.058 | 0.977 |
| 0.6 | 1.0 | 1.000 | 0.180 | 0.978 |

Breaking proportional odds does not inflate the total-DIF Type-I (0.040 and 0.055) or
cost power. The one degradation is attribution: at severe violation with true uniform
DIF present, the nonuniform test over-rejects (0.180), reading some of the violated
slope structure as nonuniform DIF. The Brant flag catches the severe regime (98%) and
is insensitive at the mild one (15%), so the card's advisory framing is right: when
`po_violation` is set, trust the total-DIF verdict and read the uniform/nonuniform
split with caution.

## S5: sample size and balance

Power at uniform DIF 1.0 (C-band input), and the unbalanced check:

| Design | Type-I (null) | Power (DIF 1.0) |
|---|---|---|
| 25 items/group | - | 0.852 |
| 50 items/group | - | 0.990 |
| 100 items/group | - | 1.000 |
| 200 items/group | - | 1.000 |
| 100 vs 33 items (3:1) | 0.048 | 0.995 |

Power is adequate from 25 items per group and effectively complete from 50. The 3:1
imbalance, roughly the shape of the SummEval demo, costs nothing: Type-I stays
nominal and power holds.

## What this study does not show

The grid varies one DGP family (cumulative logit with a normal trait, the model the
engine assumes up to the planted violations). It does not vary category counts,
threshold spacing, or non-normal trait distributions, and the bootstrap block's 50
replications per cell bound its false-positive estimates at a Monte Carlo SE of about
0.03. Cross-item within-rater dependence is stressed only through the rater intercept
SD; a judge whose severity drifts over items is outside this DGP. The calibration
curve pools two confound mechanisms by observed correlation, which is exactly how the
flag consumes it, but per-mechanism curves would sharpen the 0.2 to 0.4 band.
